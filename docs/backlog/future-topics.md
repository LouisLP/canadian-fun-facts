# Future topics

A standing idea bank for new sets. The original Fun Facts 01–22 notes are all published and
have been removed; everything queued now starts here.

Four candidates per [topic](../../src/content/topics.ts), each with three candidate slide
subjects and an **Images** note rating how hard the set will be to illustrate with
freely-licensed Wikimedia Commons files (easy / medium / hard). Image difficulty is the
main scheduling constraint — see [authoring-a-set.md](../authoring-a-set.md) step 4.

Promote an entry by writing it up as its own `docs/backlog/<slug>.md` note, then follow the
recipe. Delete the entry from this file once its set publishes.

Already published, do not re-propose: beavers, inventions, maple, filming & celebrities,
brands, language & slang, the cold, Indigenous peoples, travel, sports, weird laws,
mythical creatures, industry & tech, Canada vs Germany, heroes, music, provinces &
territories.

## Wildlife

**Polar bears** — Churchill as the self-declared polar bear capital and its tundra buggies;
the bear jail that holds problem bears before release; how sea-ice loss shifts the hunting
season. _Images: easy._

**Loons and the birds on the money** — the loon's four distinct calls and why it can't walk
on land; the Canada goose's reputation as a national menace abroad; snowy owls wintering
far south of the Arctic. _Images: easy._

**Whales of three oceans** — the St. Lawrence beluga population stranded from the Arctic;
Bay of Fundy right whales and the shipping-lane speed limits; resident orca pods off
Vancouver Island. _Images: medium — belugas and orcas are well covered, right whales less
so._

**Moose** — the size comparison that surprises everyone (taller at the shoulder than most
horses); moose–vehicle collisions and Newfoundland's introduced population; antlers shed
and regrown every single year. _Images: easy._

## Food & Drink

**Poutine** — the disputed Quebec origin story and its several claimant restaurants; why
squeaky fresh curds are non-negotiable; the regional variants and the airport-menu
globalization. _Images: easy._

**Butter tarts and the sweet canon** — the runny-vs-firm and raisin-vs-no-raisin schisms;
Nanaimo bars and their three-layer construction; the Beavertail. _Images: medium — good
butter tart and Nanaimo bar photos exist; fewer of the arguments._

**Atlantic seafood** — lobster's shift from prison food to luxury; the Grand Banks cod
moratorium and the town it emptied; PEI mussels and oyster farming. _Images: easy —
strong historical fishery archive material._

**The Canadian bar** — the Caesar and the clamato problem; rye whisky's legal definition;
Okanagan and Niagara icewine made from frozen grapes. _Images: medium._

## History

**The fur trade** — the Hudson's Bay Company chartered in 1670 and once holding a
territory the size of a continent; the point blanket and its stripes; the voyageur canoe
routes. _Images: easy — enormous public-domain archive._

**The War of 1812** — the burning of Washington in retaliation for York; Laura Secord's
walk; how both sides claim they won. _Images: easy._

**Confederation** — the 1864 Charlottetown Conference that started as a different meeting
entirely; provinces joining across 132 years, Newfoundland last in 1949; the promise of a
railway that bought British Columbia. _Images: easy._

**The Klondike gold rush** — the ton of supplies the Mounties required at the pass; Dawson
City's population spike and collapse; the stampeders who arrived after the good claims were
already staked. _Images: easy — extensive photographic record._

## Geography

**The Great Lakes** — a fifth of the world's surface fresh water; the shipwreck count and
the gales of November; the lake that is entirely within one country. _Images: easy._

**The Rockies** — the Columbia Icefield feeding three oceans; Lake Louise's glacial-flour
colour; the Burgess Shale fossils. _Images: easy._

**The Arctic archipelago** — the Northwest Passage and the ships that failed to find it;
Ellesmere and Baffin among the world's largest islands; the magnetic north pole drifting
off Canadian territory. _Images: medium._

**Fresh water and coastline** — more lake area than any other country; the longest
coastline on Earth and how fractal measurement inflates it; the Bay of Fundy's 16-metre
tides. _Images: easy._

## Weather

**Snow records** — the seasonal snowfall records out of BC's Mount Baker area; Quebec
City's annual accumulation versus its unbothered schools; snow-clearing budgets that dwarf
whole departments. _Images: easy._

**Ice storms** — the 1998 storm that collapsed transmission towers and left millions
without power for weeks; freezing rain's specific formation conditions; the pylon
photographs that became national iconography. _Images: medium — 1998 coverage is mostly
press-owned._

**Aurora borealis** — Yellowknife's near-nightly winter viewing; the solar-wind mechanism
and the colour-by-altitude rule; the aurora tourism economy. _Images: easy._

**Wind and wild extremes** — the Wreckhouse winds that blew trains off the rails in
Newfoundland; the 63 °C national temperature spread; tornado alley running through southern
Ontario and the Prairies. _Images: medium._

## Inventions

**Medical breakthroughs** — insulin isolated in Toronto and the patent sold for a dollar;
the pacemaker's Canadian prototype; the cobalt-60 cancer therapy unit. _Images: medium —
lab and portrait photography, few dramatic images._

**Communication** — the telephone's Brantford claim; the walkie-talkie; IMAX and its
oversized film stock. _Images: medium._

**Everyday things** — the paint roller; the garbage bag; the jolly jumper; instant mashed
potato. _Images: easy — product photography is abundant._

**Space and robotics** — the Canadarm on the shuttle and its station successor; Alouette,
the third satellite ever launched by any nation; the Avro Arrow's cancellation. _Images:
easy — NASA imagery is public domain._

## Culture

**Québec** — language law and the sign rules; the two referendums and the 1995 margin;
Carnaval and Bonhomme. _Images: medium._

**Multiculturalism** — the 1971 policy, a world first; the share of the population born
abroad; the neighbourhood-scale results in Toronto and Vancouver. _Images: medium._

**Screen** — the National Film Board's animation Oscars; the Vancouver and Toronto
production booms; the CanCon quota that reshaped radio. _Images: hard — modern film
stills are almost never freely licensed._

**Letters** — Anne of Green Gables' improbable popularity in Japan; the Booker and Nobel
winners; the Massey Lectures. _Images: medium._

## People

**Prime ministers** — the longest-serving one and his spiritualism; the shortest tenure;
the ones who never won a general election. _Images: easy — official portraits are public
domain._

**Scientists** — the Nobel laureates and what they won for; Marshall McLuhan's phrases that
outlived their explanations; the Hubble constant work done from Canadian observatories.
_Images: medium._

**Comedians** — the SCTV and Kids in the Hall lineage; the Canadian share of a given
decade's Hollywood comedy casts; Just for Laughs. _Images: hard — contemporary celebrity
photography is rarely free._

**Astronauts** — Roberta Bondar as the first Canadian woman in space; Chris Hadfield's
station command and the guitar; the Canadarm connection that bought the flight seats.
_Images: easy — agency imagery._

## Sports

**Lacrosse** — the official national summer sport, older than the country; the Indigenous
origins and the game's ceremonial role; the box-vs-field split. _Images: medium — good
historical engravings, fewer modern free photos._

**Curling** — the rock quarried from one Scottish island; sweeping's actual physics; the
Brier and its regional loyalties. _Images: medium._

**The Grey Cup** — a trophy older than the league that plays for it; the wider field and
three-down rule; the Grey Cup riots. _Images: medium._

**Olympic moments** — the 1976 Montreal debt paid off thirty years later; Calgary 1988 and
the borrowed bobsled team; Vancouver 2010's home-soil gold record. _Images: medium —
venue and torch photos are easier than competition shots._

## Travel

**National parks** — Banff created around a hot spring dispute; the park larger than
Switzerland; Wood Buffalo's dark-sky preserve. _Images: easy._

**The road** — the Trans-Canada's length and its one-week driving reality; the Cabot Trail;
the Dempster reaching the Arctic Ocean. _Images: easy._

**By rail** — the last-spike photograph and what it left out; the transcontinental trip
that still takes four nights; the Spiral Tunnels. _Images: easy._

**Festivals** — the Calgary Stampede's attendance; Winterlude and the skated commute on the
Rideau Canal; Just for Laughs and the Montreal jazz festival scale. _Images: medium._

## Business & Industry

**Mining** — the Sudbury basin and its meteorite-impact origin; potash reserves under
Saskatchewan; the diamond mines that opened in the 1990s Northwest Territories. _Images:
medium._

**Forestry** — the share of the country under forest; the pine beetle's range expansion;
newsprint's long dominance of the export ledger. _Images: easy._

**Energy** — the oil sands' scale and the extraction cost; Quebec's hydroelectric
overbuild and its exports; the Bay of Fundy tidal power attempts. _Images: medium._

**Agriculture** — the wheat varieties bred short-season for the Prairies; the canola name
and its Canadian coinage; the supply-management system and its quota prices. _Images:
easy._

## Oddities

**Roadside giants** — the Sudbury nickel; the several towns each claiming the world's
largest something; Vegreville's mathematically notable pysanka. _Images: easy._

**Place names** — Dildo, Newfoundland; Head-Smashed-In Buffalo Jump; the towns named after

**Unexplained sites** — the Oak Island money pit and two centuries of digging; Falcon Lake

**Records and rituals** — the longest street that isn't; the world's largest skating rink;
