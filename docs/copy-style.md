# Copy style

Rules for reader-facing prose in sets: **facts**, slide **headings**, set **titles**.

Out of scope: **alt text** (plainly descriptive, no jokes, no flourishes) and **credits** (attribution strings only).

## Facts

- Soft cap **~25 words**, one sentence. Fragments welcome; sacrifice grammar for concision ("**Basketball**: invented 1891, Almonte, Ontario").
- Lead with the surprising bit, not the setup.
- Emphasis is **bold only**: the key figure or term, max two per fact. No italics.
- Exclamation marks are allowed. Be expressive, not cute.

## Banned

- Em-dashes. Use colons, semicolons, commas, or a new fragment.
- Trailing quips and asides ("igloo engineering, but soggier", "the paperwork just took a few decades to catch up").
- "isn't just X, it's Y" constructions.
- Rhetorical questions.
- "Fun fact:" and similar lead-ins.
- AI vocabulary: delve, boasts, nestled, testament to, game-changer.

## Headings and titles

Puns and playful hooks welcome ("Dam fine engineering"). The banned list above still applies.

## Example

Before:

> Deep in **Wood Buffalo National Park**, Alberta, sits a beaver dam roughly **850 metres long** — about eight football fields. It was discovered on satellite imagery in 2007, and researchers think generations of beavers have been working on it since the 1970s.

After:

> World's longest beaver dam: **850 metres**, in Alberta's Wood Buffalo National Park, spotted via satellite in 2007.
